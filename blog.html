Inside Out and Back Again

"We have to leave," I tell my children one night.

I am a refugee.
I am an outsider.
This is what I am thinking as I huddle with children under the deck of a ship, as the waves continue to pound and crash around me.
My daughter is now scribbling on the writing pad I gave her next to me. Over the words she's written there are drawings of food. I look down at small portion of food they have given us.
Only yesterday it was ____ I think of home. Of life when
Of A black sesame sweet and banana tapioca was the only treat I managed to scrape together for her.

"It's time to go!" I remember hearing.

My daughter desperately wanted to have a say in this blog, so here.

I still miss:

  1. My papaya tree
  2. Feeling like I belong
